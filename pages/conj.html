conj : conjunct

A conjunct (conj) in a coordination structure becomes a dependent of the previous conjunct. This analysis is chosen to avoid far-distance dependencies, which makes parsing easier.


A coordination structure including "as well as".

A coordination structure ending with "etc".

A coordination structure ending with "so on".

A coordination structure including "not only" and "but also".

The noun phrase "Sam" is coordinated with the relative clause "who you met".

The preposition phrase "in America" is coordinated with the adverb phrase "world-wide".

When coordination is used for verbs, the shared arguments are connected by the secondary dependencies.


"John" is the nominal subject (nsubj) and "the book" is the direct object (dobj) of "found", "bought", and "read".

"John" is the nominal subject (nsubj) and "in 2016" is the nominal modifier (nmod) of "revised" and "made".
