sprd : small clausal predicate

A small clausal predicate (sprd) is the non-verbial predicate that completes the meaning of a small clause. We follow the flat analysis for small clauses such that the sprd relation stays the same for both active and passive constructions. In the following examples, "the genius" is labeled as sprd for both the active and passive constructions. The relationship between "John" and "the genius" is preserved by the secondary dependency ssubj, indicating the small clausal subject.
