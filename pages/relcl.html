relcl : relative clause

A relative clause (relcl) modifies the head of a noun phrase.


The relative clause "who is smart" modifying the noun "John".

The relative clause "who I wanted to meet" modifying the noun "John".

The relative clause "who I bought a book for" modifying the noun "John".

The relcl relation is used to indicate a reduced relative clause as well.


The reduced relative clause "by now a baptist" modifying the noun phrase "John".

The relative clause may contain the zero relativizer.


The reduced relative clause "by now a baptist" modifying the noun phrase "John".

The relationship between the referent of the relativizer and the predicate is preserved by a secondary dependency. Once the referent is connected to the predicate through the secondary dependency, the relativizer is assigned with the new relation rv, indicating the relativizer of this relative clause.


A relative clause whose referent is the nominal subject.

A relative clause whose referent is the direct object.

A relative clause whose referent is the dative case.
