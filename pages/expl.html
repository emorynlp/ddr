expl : expletive

An expletive (expl) is either the existential "there" or the extrapositional "it" in the subject position.


The existential "there".

When the extrapositional "it" is used, the clausal complement takes a subject relation such as the clausal subject (csubj) or the clausal topic (ctopic).


The extrapositional "it" modifying the verbal predicate "struck".

The extrapositional "it" modifying the adjectival predicate "evident".

The extrapositional "it" whose clausal complement is the infinitive clause "to understand".
