Dependencies

Two types of dependencies are used in DSR: primary dependencies and secondary dependencies. Primary dependencies (represented in solid lines) are used to generate dependency trees preserving the following four properties: single root, single head, acyclic, and connected. Secondary dependencies (represented in dotted lines) are used to generate dependency graphs consisting of deep semantic relations that are discarded from the primary dependencies to preserve the tree properties.
