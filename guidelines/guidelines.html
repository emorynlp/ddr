oprd : object predicate

The object predicate (oprd) is the non-verbial predicate of the object (or the subject for a passive construction) in a small clause. A small clause consists of a subject-predicate construction where the predicate is expressed by a non-verbial unit such as a noun phrase. We follow the flat analysis for small clauses such that the oprd relation stays the same for both active and passive constructions. In the examples above, "the genius" has the oprd relation for both the active and passive constructions. The relationship between the subject "John" and the predicate "the genius" is indicated by the open-clausal subject (xsubj).
